Day 2: Interrupts and Registers

What are Interupts?

Imagine that you are a kid helping you dad wiht a project in the garage. He is doing the work but you are bored. You stop him and ask him to get you a juicebox. He sets down his tools, gets you your juicebox, then goes back to work.

Congradulations! That is an interupt.

In our scenario, your dad was the CPU. He was following instructions and working on a big task. You tapping him on the sholder was INTERUPTING him. He stopped, took care of the time sensitive task, then he picked back up where he left off.

We will talk about 3 types of interupts today but there are tons more that you can discover on your own. The three that we will talk about today are:

  • int 0x10 (10h) - Bios Video Services
  • int 0x13 (13h) - Disk Services
  • int 0x16 (16h) - Keyboard services
